Postdoctoral Researcher – Computational Biology & Genomics

We are looking for a highly motivated postdoctoral researcher to join our lab at the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign. The position focuses on developing machine learning methods to better understand complex biological regulation from genomic and spatial data.

Project Overview

Our lab studies how gene regulation is encoded across the genome and how it varies across tissues, cell types, and disease contexts. This project will focus on building deep learning models and exploring emerging agentic AI methods to decode regulatory structures, particularly from spatial and single-cell genomics data. The work spans data integration, model development, and biological interpretation, with opportunities to collaborate across computational and experimental teams.

Basic Qualifications

  • Ph.D. in computational biology, bioinformatics, computer science, or a related field.
  • Experience working with genomics data (e.g., RNA-seq, ATAC-seq, spatial transcriptomics).
  • Proficiency in Python or R for data analysis.
  • Familiarity with machine learning or deep learning.

Additional Skills

  • Strong communication and writing skills.
  • Ability to work both independently and in a collaborative team environment.
  • A track record of scientific publications is a plus.

We welcome candidates who are excited about pushing the boundaries of AI in biology. The position is open until filled, with flexible start dates.
